Dr. A.P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Photograph of Dr.APJ Abdul Kalam

Here is a timeline of Dr. Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - Born in Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954 - Graduated from University of Madras in physics.
  • 1955- Moved to Madras to study Aero Space Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960 - After graduating from Madras Institute of Technolgy, Kalam joined in Aeronautic development establishment of DRDO as a scientist
  • 1965 - Kalam started working independently on expandable rocket project
  • 1969 - Kalam was transfered to Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first satellite launching vehicle (SLV III).
  • 1970 - Kalam also directed two projects Project Devil and Project Valiant, which sought to develop ballistic missiles from the technology of the successful SLV program.
  • 1980 - Kalam's research and educational leadership brought him great laurels and prestige in the 1980s, which prompted the government to initiate an advanced missile programme under his directorship.
  • 1990's - Kalam served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation
  • 1998 - Along with cardiologist Soma Raju, Kalam developed a low cost coronary stent, named the "Kalam-Raju Stent".
  • 2002- Kalam Served as the 11th President of India.
  • 2012 - Again along with cardiologist Soma Raju,Kalam designed a rugged tablet computer for health care in rural areas, which was named the "Kalam-Raju Tablet".

"I worked very closely with Dr. Kalam as prime minister and I greatly benefited from his advice as president of our country. His life and work will be remembered for generations to come "

Dr. Manmohan singh, Former Indian Prime Minister
